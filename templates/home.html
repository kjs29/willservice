Jack's Story

Once upon a time, there was a man named Jack who had a wife and two children. Jack was a successful business owner and had worked hard to provide for his family.

One day, Jack unexpectedly passed away due to a heart attack. His wife and children were devastated and overwhelmed with grief. As they tried to cope with their loss, they soon discovered that Jack had not made a will.

This meant that they had to go through a lengthy and complicated legal process to settle Jack's estate. They had to hire lawyers and spend a lot of time and money trying to figure out how to distribute Jack's assets and debts.

It was a difficult time for Jack's family. They were already dealing with the emotional pain of losing him, and now they had to navigate the complex legal system on top of that.

If Jack had made a will, it would have made things much easier for his family. They would have known exactly what his wishes were and how to distribute his assets without any confusion or dispute.

Jack's wife and children learned a valuable lesson about the importance of making a will. They realized that it's not just about protecting your assets, but it's also about making things easier for your loved ones after you're gone.

From that day forward, they made sure to encourage everyone they knew to make a will, no matter how young or healthy they were. They knew that it was the responsible and caring thing to do, and it would save their loved ones from unnecessary stress and hardship in the future.
Sarah's Story

Once there was a woman named Sarah who had a successful career and a loving family. Sarah was always responsible with her finances, and she knew that making a will was an important step to ensure that her family would be taken care of after she was gone.

Sarah worked with an attorney to make a will that reflected her wishes and provided for her family's future. She felt a sense of relief knowing that everything was taken care of, and she could rest easy knowing that her affairs were in order.

Years passed, and Sarah grew old gracefully, surrounded by her family and loved ones. One day, she passed away peacefully in her sleep, having lived a long and fulfilling life.

Thanks to Sarah's careful planning, her family was able to easily and quickly settle her estate. Her will provided clear instructions for how her assets should be distributed, which saved her family from any unnecessary legal battles or confusion.

Sarah's family was grateful for her foresight and responsible planning. They knew that her will was a tangible way for her to express her love and care for them, even after she was gone.

Sarah's story is a powerful example of how making a will can benefit someone and their family. It not only ensures that their wishes are carried out, but it also saves their loved ones from the stress and confusion of managing their estate.

A will is a legal document that outlines your wishes regarding how your property and assets should be distributed after your death. It also allows you to appoint a person to manage your estate (an executor) and take care of your minor children (a guardian). Creating a will is important because it ensures that your property and assets are distributed according to your wishes and can help prevent disputes among family members.

The legal requirements for a valid will vary depending on the province/state or country in which you live. In Alberta, a valid will must be in writing, signed by the testator (the person making the will) and witnessed by two people who are not beneficiaries or spouses of beneficiaries. The testator must also be of legal age and have the capacity to make a will. It’s always best to consult a lawyer to discuss what the legal requirements are for a will in your province/state or country.
